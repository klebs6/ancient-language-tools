1. ἄνθραξ -- anthrax -- coal
    /ánthracos, noun, masculine

    Definition:
    A black, carbonaceous substance formed by the partial decay or carbonization of organic matter.

    Etymology:
    From Proto-Indo-European *h₂éntlos (“coal”), from h₂en- (“to kindle, burn”).

    Connotations:
    Fire, fuel, power

    Collocations:
    ἄνθραξ ἄνωθεν - coal from above, i.e., burned-out volcanic ash

    Word Families:
    ἀνθρακολόγος (anthrakológos) - coal miner

    Synonyms and Antonyms:
    ξύλον (xylon) - wood, antonym
    λίθος (lithos) - stone, antonym

    Ancient Greek Example Sentence:
    ὁ ἄνθραξ ἔχει μεγάλην δύναμιν. (ho anthrax échei megalēn dýnamin.)
    The coal has great power.
